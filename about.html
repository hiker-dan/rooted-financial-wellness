About

The Person Behind
the Practice

Financial wellness is personal work. Here's why I do it and what I bring to the table.

Hi, I'm Daniel.

My relationship with budgeting started earlier than most. At twelve years old, I started and ran a small business—and for the first time, I experienced the real importance of managing money with intention. That early spark never went away.

After graduating from Emerson College, I worked as a freelance Assistant Director on projects throughout Los Angeles—music videos, commercials, shorts, and features. Freelance life meant unpredictable income, and it demanded that I be incredibly intentional with every dollar. That's where my passion for budgeting deepened from a practical skill into a genuine philosophy.

Today, I serve as the Senior Administrator for Events & IT at Emerson College, and I bring that same intentionality to everything I do. I've even turned my love of financial strategy into a hobby—using credit card points and miles to help cover a significant chunk of a two-week trip across Canada, from Toronto to Vancouver. That kind of financial clarity and creativity is exactly what budgeting can unlock.

I'm transparent about my privilege: I graduated without college or car loan debt, advantages that gave me a foundation many don't have. That's exactly why I care so deeply about this work—helping people gain access to the financial tools and knowledge needed to reclaim their agency in a system that isn't designed in their favor.

When I'm not coaching, you'll find me hiking and camping in California's stunning national parks, forests, and mountains. Whether you're budgeting for the first time or rebuilding your relationship with money, I'm here to walk alongside you—without judgment, without shame, and with real tools that work.

Why This Work

It's About More Than Money

Financial stress affects everything—our relationships, our mental health, our sense of possibility. But most financial advice ignores this reality. It tells you to "just save more" or "stop buying lattes" without acknowledging the emotional weight of money or the systemic barriers that make financial stability harder for some than others.

I started Rooted Financial Wellness because I believe everyone deserves access to financial tools that actually work—tools that honor your emotions, respect your circumstances, and help you build a life aligned with your values.
