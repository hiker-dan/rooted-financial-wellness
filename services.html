Pricing Philosophy

A sliding scale means you choose.

Every service I offer uses a sliding scale because I believe your current financial situation shouldn't be a barrier to getting financial support.

Each tier shows a price range. You choose what honestly fits your reality — no documentation, no questions, no judgment. I trust you.

If you can pay toward the higher end, you help keep this work accessible for others who can't. If you need the lower end right now, that's exactly what it's there for. There's no difference in the care or quality of coaching at any point on the scale.

If even the lowest listed price is out of reach, reach out anyway. Let's talk.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between financial coaching and financial advising?

Financial advisors are licensed professionals who manage investments and give specific financial product recommendations. I'm a financial coach — I help you understand your relationship with money, build practical budgets, and create habits that stick. Think of it like the difference between a personal trainer and a doctor: both matter, but for different things. Coaching is about behavior, mindset, and accountability.

How does the sliding scale actually work?

Each tier shows a price range. You look at your current financial situation honestly and choose the number that fits — no paperwork, no proof, no judgment. I trust you to know what you can afford. If you can pay toward the higher end, you're helping keep this work accessible for others. If you need the lower end, that's exactly what it's there for.

What if I need to cancel or reschedule a session?

Life happens. I ask for 24-hour notice when possible, but I understand things come up. We'll find a time that works.

Is coaching worth it if I'm already in debt?

Especially then. Understanding the emotional and behavioral patterns that got you here is as important as the practical steps out. We work on both, together.

What happens when my bundle ends?

That's a great conversation to have toward the end of your program. Some clients feel ready to fly solo; others choose to continue with individual sessions or a new bundle. There's no pressure either way — the goal is your independence, not your dependence.

Do I need to pay for YNAB?

Not during our time together. YNAB access is included at no extra cost for the duration of your coaching engagement—whether that's through a free trial or shared access on my account, depending on your tier. After coaching ends, YNAB is $109/year (~$9.08/month) or $14.99 month-to-month if you'd like to keep using it. By then, you'll know whether it works for you. For more details on how access works per tier, visit my Approach page.

Do you offer payment plans?

Yes. The Rooting bundle can be split into 2 monthly installments, and Flourishing into 3. No additional cost — just a lower barrier to getting started.

How do I know which tier is right for me?

That's exactly what the free Discovery Call is for. We'll talk through where you are, what you're trying to achieve, and I'll give you my honest recommendation. No hard sell.
