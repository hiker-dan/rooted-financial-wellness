Resources

What I Watch, Listen to,
and Recommend

While there are plenty of valuable books on financial wellness and budgeting, I personally am always watching YouTube videos or listening to podcasts as my preferred way of taking in information. Here is a curated list of some of my favorite channels, podcasts, and free resources that have shaped how I think about money.

Recommended YouTube Channels

The Channels I Watch

YNAB ▶

YNAB Official

One of my favorite aspects of YNAB is how many resources they provide entirely for free. They have a massive library of videos offering insights, tips, and tricks for using their software. Whether you want to attempt it on your own or use it as a supplement after our sessions, their official channel has a lot to offer.

Visit Channel →
SJ ▶

Spencer Johnson

It's incredibly important to start any conversation about credit cards by establishing the importance of paying them off in full and on time every single month—paying off credit card debt is a goal I'm eager to help clients focus on and achieve. But this is, to me, the fun section of finances. I enjoy learning about the perks and advantages of handling credit cards responsibly, and Spencer's videos offer a really clear breakdown of the nuances of the credit card world.

Visit Channel →
DB ▶

Daniel Braun

Daniel Braun was one of the first credit card and personal finance channels I stumbled across. His videos explain topics in a clear, concise, and easy-to-digest format—perfect for learning about the best high-yield savings accounts or basic budgeting strategies. Be sure to check out his Personal Finance playlist!

Visit Channel →
Podcasts I Listen To

Worth a Listen

🎙️

Budget Nerds with Ben & Ernie

One of the official podcasts by YNAB—it's definitely in the weeds for budget nerds, but an enjoyable listen nonetheless! Ben and Ernie nerd out about all things YNAB to help you get good with money. They share helpful money tips, reflect on their own YNAB journeys, and celebrate your YNAB wins.

Listen →
🎙️

Washington Wise by Charles Schwab

I won't lie, this podcast can be fairly dry—but sometimes that's what conversations around economics and investing need to be. Host Mike Townsend looks at news and policies from Washington that impact investors from a non-partisan lens, and offers actionable ideas for your portfolio.

Listen →
🎙️

Planet Money (NPR)

What is any podcast list without an NPR show? NPR's long-running explainer podcast explores the forces that shape our lives and how it all ties back to the economy. Don't just understand the economy—understand the larger systems your finances exist inside.

Listen →
🎙️

Frequent Miler on the Air

This one is the deep dive for credit card points and miles nerds. The same disclaimer applies: credit cards are a tool that should always be paid off in full and on time every month. Frequent Miler is all about getting the most out of the points and miles game.

Listen →
Free Resources Online

Websites Worth Bookmarking

Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)

The government agency that actually advocates for consumers. They offer a multitude of completely free tools to help with personal finance, including how to understand credit reports and how to tackle debt, just to name a few. The CFPB Your Money, Your Goals Toolkit is a fantastic place to start—download the PDF and search for the topic you need help with!

Your Money, Your Goals Toolkit →

YNAB Free Workshops

YNAB offers entirely free live workshops! If one-on-one coaching doesn't feel like the right fit at this moment, these workshops are a great way to get familiar with YNAB on your own.
